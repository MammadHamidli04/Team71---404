For teaching coding different platforms are being used, and we experienced teaching Code.org during Hour of code. Hour of code is an hour of introduction to computer science. During this project each group had to choose a community, and then organize this event there. In the teaching process a maze puzzle was used and before teaching we ourselves completed tasks in code.org and gained experience. The main idea of this project is to teach kids easy programming skills and algorithms, and also encourage kids to learn computer science. To read our experience and what we did during this event scroll down.

To start about our experience, every groupwork starts with finding time suitable for each team member. For us it was one of the hardest stages. After this our challenges continued with finding school. After talking with several schools we choose to go to Baku Young Talents Lyceum. We choose younger -aged kids because they have lots of time to improve their knowledge.

During the hour of code first we talked about ADA university. After informing about ADA we started explaining the topic. Via providing slides prepared by us, also via solving questions from maze puzzle kids understood the topic perfectly. For them to better understand we helped them in case if kids had questions. For creating a competitive environment, we gave kids exact tasks and who completed first had a chance to explain questions to classmates. Though it was a great experience, of course we had some struggles too. We had struggles with kids interested in programming. They tried to solve everything fast and then not listen to us. But we could solve these problems, keep the attention of students, even make them raise both hands or clap to keep focused. In addition, in code.org angry birds games and other visual effects are used so the colour and graphics caught their attention. The kids' knowledge about algorithms have improved a lot till the end of the lesson. Most have completed all parts and others have explained all parts in detail. To summarize everything, we are glad that we could impact society positively, and maybe could encourage new generations to learn programming.

To conclude we want to thank, staff of Baku Young Talents Lyceum for the provided support and permission to hold lessons at their school.
